What's going on with the Switch 2 leaks?

Matthew Sutton, Sole Contributor

January 09, 2025

Leaks about the Nintendo Switch 2 have been surfacing everywhere for weeks now with more big news dropping this morning. OnLeaks, in collaboration with 91mobiles, shared a 360-degree render of the Nintendo Switch 2, telling us the following:

  • The new model features an 8.4-inch screen, a considerable upgrade from the original Switch's 6.2-inch display.
  • The next Switch will retain a 3.5mm headphone jack on top, along with a USB Type-C charging port next to it—a welcome change for anyone like me who found charging the Switch while using it in handheld mode frustrating.
  • The kickstand will feature a 'U' shaped kickstand for playing on table-top mode.

This isn't the only news we've gotten lately:

  • Today a user on Reddit posted a picture of a new SKU indicating that the Switch 2 will support MicroSD express cards which are faster than the cards currently supported in the original Switch.
  • Last week, alleged images of the new Joy-Cons were leaked online showing that the new controllers will have a magnetic connection rather than sliding together on a rail.
  • Digital Foundry did a segment on the new Switch 2 motherboard.
  • At CES, Genki showed off a replica model of the Switch 2 that is said to feature the exact dimensions of the upcoming console.

The Switch 2 appears to be a more powerful, slightly larger version of the original Switch, and I’m totally fine with that. I was a bit worried Nintendo might throw a curveball, as they often do, and we’d end up with something wild like a new Power Glove with a screen in the palm. It's really strange for Nintendo to keep the exact same appearance for a follow-up console - even the Wii U, despite sharing a similar name, looked completely different from the Wii.

I have no doubt that this successor will be a massive success like its predecessor, which is now the third best-selling console of all time with 146 million units sold. I’m not sure what Nintendo is waiting for, but hopefully, a Nintendo Direct is around the corner so we can finally see the real thing. Also, Nintendo, please let me play Splatoon 3 at 120 FPS. Thanks in advance!
