Nintendo Switch 2 will be getting Xbox games according to leaker

NateTheHate, a reliable leaker and podcaster, claimed Halo: The Master Chief Collection and Microsoft flight Simulator 2024  will be coming to the Nintendo Switch 2.


These games are also rumored to be releasing on the PlayStation 5 despite being Xbox-exclusive.


Microsoft releasing games on other platforms isn't surprising since they've already begun releasing other games on PlayStation 5 such as Pentiment, Hi-Fi Rush, Sea of Thieves, and Grounded.


It was also announced recently that Microsoft will be releasing Doom: The Dark Ages for PS5 and will also release a PS5 version of Indiana Jones and the Great Circle.


This appears to just be a continuation of that strategy, but it is noteworthy that the Nintendo Switch 2 will even be able to run these games.


Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 is a pretty hefty game, requiring at least a GeForce GTX 970 to play it at a minimum.


This tells us that the Switch 2 is either going to be decently powerful, or there is going to be a serious amount of optimization to get it running at a decent frame rate.


This news also comes at the same time that Nintendo has been dealing with numerous Nintendo Switch 2 leaks, which Nintendo acknowledged as "Not Official".


These leaks included a 360 degree render of the Switch 2, telling us the following:

  • The new model features an 8.4-inch screen, a considerable upgrade from the original Switch’s 6.2-inch display.
  • The next Switch will retain a 3.5mm headphone jack on top, along with a USB Type-C charging port next to it—a welcome change for anyone like me who found charging the Switch while using it in handheld mode frustrating.
  • The kickstand will feature a ‘U’ shaped kickstand for playing on table-top mode.
  • Yesterday, a user on Reddit posted a picture of a new SKU indicating that the Switch 2 will support MicroSD express cards which are faster than the cards currently supported in the original Switch.
  • Last week, alleged images of the new Joy-Cons were leaked online showing that the new controllers will have a magnetic connection rather than sliding together on a rail.
  • Digital Foundry did a segment on the new Switch 2 motherboard.
  • At CES, Genki showed off a replica model of the Switch 2 that is said to feature the exact dimensions of the upcoming console.
